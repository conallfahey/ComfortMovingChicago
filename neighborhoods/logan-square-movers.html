Moving to Logan Square

Logan Square isn't just another Chicago ZIP code; it’s a neighborhood of historic boulevards, diagonal one-ways, and beautiful (but narrow) vintage greystones.

Whether you’re moving into a modern mid-rise near the California Blue Line or a century-old walk-up near Palmer Square, the logistics of a Logan Square move require a local game plan.

1. The Logan Square "Parking Game"

In many Chicago neighborhoods, you can "wing it" with parking. In Logan Square, that’s a $150 ticket waiting to happen.

  • The Milwaukee Ave Challenge: If your new spot is near the "Mega Mall" redevelopment or the heart of the Square, street parking is virtually non-existent.
  • Permit Requirements: Most residential streets here (like Rockwell or Whipple) require a Zone 102 or Zone 118 permit.
  • Our Local Tip: We recommend applying for a Temporary "No Parking" sign via the 32nd or 35th Ward Alderman’s office at least 72 hours in advance. If you don't have these, we may have to double-park or use an alley, which can add "long-carry" fees to your move.

2. Navigating the "Logan Square Labyrinth"

This neighborhood is famous for its diagonal streets and six-way intersections (like the Milwaukee/Logan/Kedzie nexus).

  • Tight Turns: Driving a large commercial truck through the circle at the Illinois Centennial Monument is an art form. Our drivers are trained to navigate these specific "bottlenecks" to ensure we arrive on time, even during the Sunday Logan Square Farmers Market rush.
  • Vintage Walk-ups: Moving into a classic two-flat? Many of these buildings have "coffin turns" in the stairwells. We specialize in maneuvering oversized sofas through these tight landings without scuffing your original hardwood trim.
