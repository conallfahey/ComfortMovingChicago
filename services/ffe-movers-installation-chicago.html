What FF&E Means

Furniture, Fixtures, and Equipment We Move and Set Up

In real move-day terms, FF&E, also written as FFE, means furniture, fixtures, and equipment: movable commercial pieces that need to be delivered, assembled, placed, or reset inside your space.

Office Furniture

Desks, chairs, conference tables, standing desks, workstations, cabinets, shelving, monitors, and shared office furniture.

Retail and Showroom Fixtures

Shelving, racks, display tables, mirrors, storage pieces, fixtures, and back-room equipment.

Restaurant and Hospitality

Tables, chairs, benches, host stands, patio furniture, lounge furniture, and room-by-room hospitality setup.

Studios, Clinics, and Salons

Waiting-room furniture, treatment-room furniture, mirrors, storage, shelving, chairs, and small equipment.

Model Units and STRs

Furniture delivery, bed frames, dressers, couches, dining sets, decor placement, and packaging removal.

Amenity Spaces

Lobby seating, coworking rooms, lounge furniture, mailroom furniture, fitness-room pieces, and shared-space refreshes.

How It Works

Our FF&E Process

1. Send the scope

Send the address, target date, item list, photos, furniture links, floor plans, or delivery details so we can quote the crew size, timing, and equipment needed.

2. Confirm building rules

Chicago commercial buildings often require COIs, loading dock reservations, freight elevator windows, security check-ins, and after-hours access approval.

3. Move, protect, and stage

We protect furniture, carry items carefully, and stage pieces by room, area, or priority.

4. Assemble and place

We assemble common furniture, reattach parts, level pieces where needed, and place items according to your plan.

5. Final walkthrough

Before we leave, we check placement, reassembly, remaining items, and obvious cleanup needs.
