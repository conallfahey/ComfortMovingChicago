## Common Piano Moving Questions

## Do I need to tune my piano after the move?

Yes, we recommend waiting 2-3 weeks after the move for the piano to acclimate to the new environment's humidity and temperature before having it tuned.

## How do you protect my floors?

We use neoprene floor runners, plywood for heavy rolling, and specialized piano dollies with rubber wheels to ensure your hardwood or tile floors remain scratch-free.

## Can you move a piano up stairs?

Yes, we are experienced in moving uprights up and down stairs. For grand pianos, stairs can be more challenging and depend on the landing size; we will assess this during the quote process.

## Common Questions

## Is my piano insured during the move?

Yes, we carry full liability insurance and specific coverage for high-value items like pianos. Your instrument is protected from start to finish.

## Do you tune the piano after moving?

We specialize in moving, not tuning. However, we recommend waiting 2-3 weeks after a move for the piano to acclimate to its new environment before having it tuned.

## Can you move a piano up stairs?

Yes, but it depends on the staircase width and turns. We'll ask for photos or measurements beforehand to ensure a safe move.

## Common Questions

## Do you move all types of pianos?

Yes, we have the equipment and expertise to move everything from spinets and uprights to baby grands and concert grand pianos.

## Will my piano need tuning after the move?

While we take every precaution to move your piano gently, changes in temperature, humidity, and the physical movement often mean a tuning is recommended a few weeks after the move once it settles in the new environment.

## How many movers are required?

It depends on the type of piano and the access (stairs, elevators). Typically, an upright requires 2-3 movers, while a grand piano may require 3-4 specialists.
