# The 1901 Project in Chicago: What It Could Mean for the West Loop & Near West Side | Comfort Moving Chicago

If you live in Chicago, especially near the [West Loop](https://comfortmovingchicago.com/neighborhoods/west-loop-movers.html) or Near West Side, there is a major development project worth watching closely. The 1901 Project is a long-term redevelopment plan centered around the United Center campus at 1901 W. Madison Street, and it could significantly change the surrounding area over the next decade.

For residents, renters, property owners, and anyone planning a move, this is the kind of project that can shape neighborhood growth, traffic, housing demand, and local logistics. Official project materials describe it as a $7 billion, multi-phase redevelopment spanning more than 55 acres around the United Center.

## What Is the 1901 Project?

The project is backed by the ownership groups behind the Chicago Bulls and Chicago Blackhawks, and the vision goes far beyond the arena itself. Plans call for a large mixed-use district with housing, hospitality, retail, entertainment, and major public open space. The goal is to turn the current sea of parking lots around the United Center into a more connected, walkable neighborhood on Chicago’s West Side.

### At-a-Glance

- Scale: $7B, 55+ acres around the United Center
- Entertainment: 6,000-seat music hall (planned)
- Hospitality: 233-room hotel (planned)
- Housing: Up to 9,463 residential units over time (reported)
- Public space: 25 acres of parks and open space (project materials)

From a neighborhood perspective, the location is a big reason this development stands out. As those areas continue to evolve, large projects nearby can have ripple effects on housing demand, traffic flow, local business activity, and the overall feel of the neighborhood.

### What this can change (practically) on move day

- Event traffic near the United Center (especially evenings and weekends)
- Construction detours and shifting parking rules
- Busier loading zones and tighter curb space
- More high-rise move-ins with elevator reservation requirements

The project has already moved through major city approvals. The Chicago Plan Commission approved the proposal in January 2025, and the Chicago City Council approved it in February 2025, allowing the redevelopment to move forward in phases.
