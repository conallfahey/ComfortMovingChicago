# How Much to Tip Movers in Chicago: A Complete Guide | Comfort Moving Chicago

Tipping is a common practice in the service industry, and moving is no exception. While not mandatory, tipping is a great way to show appreciation for the hard work your moving crew puts in. But how much should you tip? Here is our guide to tipping movers in Chicago.

## 1. Is Tipping Required?

Tipping is never required, but it is standard practice in the moving industry. Movers perform physically demanding work, often navigating stairs, tight corners, and heavy furniture. A tip is a gesture of thanks for their effort and care.

## 2. How Much Should You Tip?

A general rule of thumb is to tip $5 to $10 per mover, per hour. Alternatively, you can tip a percentage of the total move cost, typically around 15-20% divided among the crew. For a full-day move, $40-$60 per mover is considered a generous tip.

## 3. Factors to Consider

Consider the complexity of your move. If you have a lot of heavy items, several flights of stairs, or difficult access, you might want to tip on the higher end. The quality of service should also be a factor”did they arrive on time, handle your items with care, and work efficiently?

## 4. When to Tip

It's best to tip at the end of the move, once everything is unloaded and you've had a chance to inspect your belongings. Hand the tip to each mover individually if possible, or give the total amount to the foreman to distribute.

## 5. Non-Monetary Tips

In addition to cash, providing cold water, sports drinks, and snacks throughout the day is highly appreciated. Buying lunch for the crew (like pizza or sandwiches) is another great way to say thanks.

### Tipping Etiquette

- Have cash on hand in small bills so you can tip each mover individually.
- If a mover goes above and beyond, feel free to tip them a little extra.
- Always offer water, especially on hot summer days.
- A sincere "thank you" and a positive review are also valuable ways to show appreciation.
