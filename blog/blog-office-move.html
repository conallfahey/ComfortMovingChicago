Office Move Planning Guide

Minimize downtime and ensure a smooth transition for your business.

November 10, 2023 Conall Fahey

Moving an office is a complex logistical challenge that requires careful planning to avoid disrupting your business operations. From IT infrastructure to employee morale, there are many factors to consider. Here is our guide to planning a successful office move.

1. Appoint a Move Manager

Designate one person internally to oversee the entire moving process. This "Move Manager" will be the primary point of contact for the moving company, employees, and vendors, ensuring clear communication and decision-making.

2. Create a Floor Plan

Before moving day, create a detailed floor plan of your new office. Decide where each employee, department, and piece of equipment will go. This will speed up the unloading process and help employees settle in faster.

3. Communicate with Employees

Keep your team informed about the move timeline and expectations. Provide them with packing guidelines for their personal workspaces and encourage them to purge unnecessary files and items before the move.

4. Hire Professional Office Movers

Office moves involve heavy furniture, sensitive electronics, and confidential files. Hire a moving company with experience in commercial relocations (like us!) to ensure everything is handled safely and efficiently.

5. Update Business Information

Don't forget to update your address on your website, Google Business Profile, social media, invoices, and email signatures. Notify clients, vendors, and partners well in advance of the move.
